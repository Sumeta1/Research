Authentic Research sites

for Grade 11 and 12 students

If you are a grade 11 or grade 12 student, I'm sure you are searching for a website with trusted content, and just for that, we are here to aid your needs.

Here are the sites that we recommend

Google Scholar

Google Scholar is a free academic search engine that indexes scholarly literature across various disciplines and formats. It provides access to peer-reviewed journal articles, scholarly books, conference papers, theses, dissertations, preprints, abstracts, technical reports, court opinions, and patents.

Features and Functionality

1. Search Capabilities: Google Scholar uses a web crawler to identify and index scholarly content. It prioritizes sources from academic domains like.edu and.gov, providing more relevant results for scholarly research compared to general web searches.
2. Citation Analysis: It offers features like "Cited by" to view articles that have cited a specific publication, and "Related articles" to find closely related works. This allows researchers to track the impact of their work and explore related research.
3. Citation Tools: Google Scholar provides citations in various academic styles (e.g., MLA, APA) and allows users to export bibliographic data to reference management software.
4. Full-Text Access: While some results may require payment or institutional access, Google Scholar often links to freely available full-text versions of articles through open access repositories or institutional library subscriptions.

Jstor

A subscription-based digital library with a vast archive of academic journals, books, and primary sources. It excels in providing access to back issues of journals, making it invaluable for historical research. Access is often through institutional subscriptions.

​ -Strengths: High-quality, peer-reviewed content, extensive historical archives, robust search capabilities within its curated collection.
​-Weaknesses: Requires subscriptions (often institutional), limited coverage of recent publications compared to constantly updated databases.
-Ideal Research Applications: Historical research, in-depth analysis of established fields, accessing back issues of journals, exploring classic texts.

DeepDyve

A pay-per-view or subscription-based platform offering access to a large number of scholarly articles. It's convenient for accessing individual articles but can be costly for extensive research.

-Strengths: Easy access to individual articles, broad coverage across disciplines.
-Weaknesses: Can be expensive, paywall access limits extensive research, quality of included articles varies.
-Ideal Research Applications: Accessing specific articles when institutional access is unavailable, supplementing research from other sources.

BASE(Bielefield Academic Search Engine)

A unique search engine focusing solely on open-access scholarly resources. It indexes millions of documents from various repositories and publishers, making it a valuable tool for researchers with limited access to subscription databases.

-Strengths: Focus on open-access resources, wide coverage, free access.
-Weaknesses: Quality control can vary among open-access sources, may miss some high-quality research behind paywalls.
-Ideal Research Applications: Finding freely available research materials, supplementing research from subscription databases, identifying open-access publications.

Directory of Open Access Journals(DOAJ)

A curated directory of open-access, peer-reviewed journals. It acts as a quality filter, ensuring the journals listed meet certain criteria.

-Strengths: Focus on quality-assured open-access journals, facilitates access to reliable research.
-Weaknesses: Not all open-access journals are included; coverage is not exhaustive.
-Ideal Research Applications: Finding reliable, freely available research, ensuring the quality of open-access sources.

ERIC (Educational Resources Information Center)

A specialized database devoted to education-related research, providing access to a vast collection of peer-reviewed literature, research reports, and educational resources.

-Strengths: Curated collection specifically for education research, extensive coverage of educational topics.
-Weaknesses: Limited to the field of education.
-Ideal Research Applications: Education-related research, pedagogy, educational policy, educational technology.
